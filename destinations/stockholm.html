About Stockholm & the Archipelago

The Stockholm Archipelago extends from the city's eastern edge deep into the Baltic Sea, comprising approximately 30,000 islands, islets, and skerries. The inner archipelago, closest to the city, is densely wooded with oak and pine. The outer archipelago, further east, is largely treeless — low granite rocks polished smooth by glaciation, with isolated fishing communities, historic lighthouses, and shallow channels navigable only in small vessels.

Stockholm itself, built across 14 islands at the point where Lake Mälaren meets the Baltic, is one of the most architecturally coherent capitals in Europe. The Gamla Stan (Old Town), constructed on three small islands in the 13th century, contains Sweden's largest collection of medieval architecture. The Djurgården island, a former royal hunting ground adjacent to the city centre, houses the Vasa Museum (home to a perfectly preserved 17th-century warship), the ABBA Museum, and the open-air Skansen cultural museum.

Faraway & Fine's Stockholm programme uses the SY Isfjord — a 28-metre sailing ketch purpose-built for Swedish waters — for all archipelago passages. The itinerary divides time between Stockholm city (based at a private apartment on Djurgårdsvägen) and the outer archipelago, with three nights anchored off the islands of Sandhamn and Utö.

Getting There

Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN) is served by direct flights from London Heathrow (British Airways, SAS), London Gatwick (Norwegian), and Manchester (SAS). Flight time from London is approximately 2 hours 20 minutes. Faraway & Fine arranges all transfers from Arlanda to the Djurgårdsvägen apartment — approximately 40 minutes by private car.
