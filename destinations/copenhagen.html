About Copenhagen

Copenhagen is the capital of Denmark and sits on the eastern coast of the island of Zealand, connected to the island of Amager by several bridges and to Malmö, Sweden, by the Øresund Bridge. The city has a population of approximately 794,000 in the municipality and around 1.3 million in the greater metropolitan area. It is consistently ranked among the most liveable cities in the world, and since the early 2000s has become the global epicentre of the New Nordic food movement — a culinary philosophy founded on hyper-local, seasonal, and foraged ingredients.

The Frederiksstaden district, where Faraway & Fine's private townhouse is located, was planned by King Frederick V in the mid-18th century as a Baroque quarter befitting a European capital. The Bredgade — the main street of the district — is lined with rococo and neoclassical palaces, embassies, and private mansions. The Designmuseum Denmark, housed in a former royal hospital at the end of Bredgade, contains the world's largest collection of Danish design and applied arts.

North Zealand — the area north of Copenhagen along the Øresund coast — contains three of Denmark's most significant cultural sites: Kronborg Castle (the setting for Shakespeare's Hamlet, a UNESCO World Heritage Site), the Louisiana Museum of Modern Art at Humlebæk, and the Frederiksborg Castle at Hillerød. All three are accessible within 45 minutes of central Copenhagen by private car.

New Nordic Food in Copenhagen

The New Nordic movement was codified in 2004 with the publication of the New Nordic Manifesto, signed by twelve leading Scandinavian chefs. Its principles — celebrating Nordic terroir, foraging, fermentation, and the seasons — have influenced restaurant cooking worldwide. Copenhagen today has 24 Michelin-starred restaurants, more per capita than any other Scandinavian city. Faraway & Fine's Cultural Director has personal relationships with the front-of-house teams at several of these restaurants and can arrange access at short notice for guests.

The Torvehallerne covered market, opened in 2011, brings together the finest Danish food producers under two glass-roofed halls near Nørreport station. It is where Copenhagen's chefs shop, and a morning visit with Faraway & Fine's Cultural Director is one of the highlights of the Copenhagen programme.
