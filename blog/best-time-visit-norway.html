The Best Time
to Visit Norway

Published October 2024

Norway is a country that changes so dramatically between seasons that choosing the wrong month can mean missing the experience you came for entirely. The northern lights and the midnight sun are separated by just six months — and are mutually exclusive. Fjord hiking requires dry, warm weather. Ski touring requires deep snow. Dog-sledding requires frozen ground. This guide breaks it down month by month.

Winter (November–March): Darkness, Aurora, and Snow

The Norwegian winter is, for many travellers, the most atmospheric season. Above the Arctic Circle — which crosses Norway at around 66.5°N, taking in the Lofoten Islands, Tromsø, and all of Finnmark — the sun disappears below the horizon entirely for several weeks around the winter solstice. This is the polar night, or mørketid ("dark time" in Norwegian), a period that some residents find oppressive but that visitors increasingly seek out for its extraordinary quality of light.

The best Faraway & Fine winter experiences are the Tromsø Aurora Wilderness Camp (available November–March, from £5,200 per person for 6 nights) and the Lofoten Islands Winter Expedition (January only, from £5,600 per person for 8 nights). The fjord yacht programmes are suspended in winter — the MV Nordlys is maintained at Ålesund between October and April.

January

−8°C at elevation · Best aurora month

Peak aurora season. Coldest temperatures. Lofoten at its most dramatic and most deserted. Statistically the clearest skies of the winter.

February

−6°C · Aurora season continuing

Similar to January but with noticeably longer days beginning to return. Valentine's period creates accommodation demand — book early.

March

−3°C · Equinox aurora enhancement

Days lengthening rapidly. Geomagnetic activity increases around the equinox. Late March can produce exceptional aurora displays.

November

−1°C · Season opening

Aurora season begins. First significant snowfall. Quiet — before the peak winter travel season. Good value on accommodation.

Spring (April–May): Transition and Waterfalls

April and May are transitional months in Norway — the snow is melting, the days are rapidly lengthening, and the waterfalls fed by snowmelt are at their most spectacular. The Geirangerfjord's Seven Sisters waterfall reaches peak flow in May, when snowmelt from the surrounding peaks combines with spring rainfall. The MV Nordlys returns to service in May, and the first fjord yacht departures of the season offer the combination of snow still on the peaks above and wildflowers already blooming in the valleys below.

Note that April fjord departures are not available — the MV Nordlys undergoes its annual maintenance inspection in April and is not available for charter until the first week of May.

Summer (June–August): Midnight Sun and Peak Season

Norwegian summer is extraordinary. Above the Arctic Circle, the sun does not set between late May and late July — it simply moves across the sky in a great circle, dipping towards the northern horizon at midnight but never disappearing. This is the midnight sun, and the quality of light it produces — horizontal, golden, and seemingly endless — is unlike anything elsewhere in the world. The fjords at midnight in June, with the sun still lighting the mountain peaks above, are one of the great sights of European travel.

The fjord yacht season peaks in June and July. All three Faraway & Fine vessels are in operation. Prices for the Geirangerfjord programme in peak summer are from £8,400 per person — slightly higher than the shoulder months of May and September.

Autumn (September–October): Colour and Quiet

September is many experienced Norway travellers' favourite month. The midnight sun has ended, the main tourist season is winding down, and the birch forests that cover Norway's valley sides turn brilliant yellow and orange. The fjords are quieter than in summer, the light is lower and more dramatic, and the first aurora of the season becomes possible from mid-September. The MV Nordlys completes its final fjord departure of the year in late September.
