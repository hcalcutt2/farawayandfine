Pricing & Booking

How much do Faraway & Fine journeys cost?
Prices vary by destination and season. As a general guide, our Scandinavian experiences range from approximately £3,500 per person for a five-night cultural immersion to £9,000 per person for a seven-night private yacht charter. Prices are based on two guests sharing. Solo travellers are welcome and are charged a single supplement of 40% rather than the full double rate. All prices include accommodation, all described activities, most meals, and 24/7 Travel Director support. International flights are not included.
What deposit is required to secure a booking?
A deposit of 20% of the total journey cost is required to confirm a booking. The balance is due 90 days before departure. For journeys booked within 90 days of departure, the full amount is due at the time of booking. Deposits are non-refundable in the event of cancellation by the guest.
Can I book a journey as a solo traveller?
Yes. Solo travellers are welcome on all Faraway & Fine programmes. A single supplement of 40% applies to all solo bookings on our yacht programmes, reflecting the cost of a private cabin. For land-based programmes (Copenhagen, Stockholm city stays, aurora cabins), solo travellers occupy a double room or cabin alone and pay a 35% supplement.
Are there any deals or discounts available?
Yes — see our Deals & Offers page for current promotions. We typically offer early booking discounts of 10–20% on journeys booked more than six months in advance, seasonal specials on the aurora programme in January and February, and occasional last-minute availability at reduced rates. We also offer a loyalty discount of 10% on all return bookings.

The Journeys

How many guests travel together on a Faraway & Fine journey?
All Faraway & Fine journeys are private — you will never share a vessel or property with another group. Our vessel programmes carry a maximum of eight guests, though most operate with four to six. Our land-based programmes (aurora cabins, city stays) are arranged exclusively for your group, which can range from two to six guests.
What is the minimum age for Faraway & Fine journeys?
The minimum age for all Faraway & Fine expedition programmes is 16 years. For our cultural immersion programmes (Copenhagen, Stockholm, Gothenburg), children aged 12 and above are welcome. We do not operate family-specific programmes — all itineraries are designed for adults, though families with older children are warmly welcomed. Please speak to your Travel Director about specific requirements.
Are the itineraries fixed or flexible?
All Faraway & Fine itineraries are starting points, not contracts. Before departure, your Travel Director will discuss the itinerary with you in detail and make adjustments based on your preferences, fitness level, and interests. During the journey, the captain and guide retain the right to modify the itinerary based on weather, sea conditions, or wildlife sightings — we may extend time in a location if conditions are exceptional, or substitute an activity if conditions make it unsafe.
What level of fitness is required?
Most Faraway & Fine experiences require a moderate level of fitness — the ability to walk for several hours over uneven terrain, board and disembark from a vessel using a ladder, and tolerate cold weather. Our Arctic and expedition programmes (Tromsø, Lofoten, Geirangerfjord) are more demanding and require guests to be comfortable with physical activity in cold conditions. Our cultural programmes (Copenhagen, Gothenburg, Stockholm city) have no significant fitness requirements. Guests with specific health conditions or mobility considerations should discuss these with their Travel Director before booking.

The Northern Lights

Can you guarantee northern lights sightings?
No — nobody can guarantee natural phenomena, and we would be dishonest if we tried. What we can tell you is that our aurora cabin programme above Tromsø, with its elevated position above the cloud inversion layer, significantly improves the odds compared to standard Tromsø accommodation. Based on our records since 2021, guests on our six-night programme have seen visible aurora on at least two nights in approximately 78% of stays. In 14% of stays they have seen aurora on five or six nights. In 8% of stays they have seen none.
What elevation are the aurora cabins at?
The aurora cabins are positioned at approximately 400 metres above sea level on a south-facing hillside 14 kilometres from Tromsø city centre. At this elevation, the cabins sit above the cloud inversion layer that forms over the fjord on most winter nights. The exact elevation varies slightly across the three cabins — the highest is at 420 metres, the lowest at 380 metres.
What months are best for the northern lights?
Aurora is possible any time the sky is dark enough — at Tromsø, this means from late October to mid-March. There is no significant difference in aurora frequency between months within this window. January is generally considered the best month because it tends to have the most stable, clear weather at Tromsø. See our complete northern lights guide for a detailed breakdown.
