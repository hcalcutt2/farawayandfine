Western Norway · Fjord Yacht Charter

Geirangerfjord &
Nærøyfjord Private Yacht

7 Nights · May to September

Faraway & Fine has secured exclusive seasonal use of the MV Nordlys — a 38-metre expedition ketch built in Bergen in 1987 and fully restored in 2022. The vessel accommodates up to six guests with a crew of five, including a Nordic chef who sources ingredients daily from fjordside farms and fishing boats.

The route covers Geirangerfjord (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), the near-vertical walls of Nærøyfjord (Europe's narrowest fjord), and the remote Sunnylvsfjord. A private hiking guide leads an ascent to the Seven Sisters viewpoint, accessible only on foot from the water.

  • Exclusive use of MV Nordlys for up to 6 guests
  • Full crew: captain, first mate, engineer, chef, hiking guide
  • All meals using daily-sourced Norwegian produce and seafood
  • Private guided hike to Seven Sisters viewpoint
  • Daily kayaking from the yacht in protected fjord waters
  • 24/7 Faraway & Fine Travel Director contact
£8,400 per person · based on 4 guests

Northern Norway · Arctic Wilderness

Tromsø Aurora
Wilderness Experience

6 Nights · November to March

Faraway & Fine operates three private glass-roofed wilderness cabins on a hillside 14 kilometres above Tromsø, at an elevation of 420 metres — above the light pollution of the city and positioned for optimal aurora viewing. Each cabin sleeps two and is staffed by a dedicated local guide throughout the stay.

The programme includes a full-day dog-sledding expedition across the Lyngen Alps with a Sami musher, a private snowshoeing journey to a frozen waterfall lit only by headtorch, and an evening with a Sami family at their traditional lavvu tent over reindeer stew and joik singing.

  • 6 nights in a private glass-roofed aurora cabin (sleeps 2)
  • Full-day dog-sledding across the Lyngen Alps with Sami musher
  • Private snowshoeing to a frozen waterfall by headtorch
  • Evening with a Sami family: reindeer stew and joik singing
  • Northern lights wake-up service throughout the stay
  • Return transfers from Tromsø Airport (TOS)
£5,200 per person · based on 2 guests

Denmark · Cultural Immersion

Copenhagen &
North Zealand

5 Nights · Year-round

Faraway & Fine's Copenhagen programme is based in a privately managed townhouse on Bredgade in the Frederiksstaden district — a 19th-century neoclassical street two minutes' walk from the Designmuseum Denmark. The property sleeps four and is arranged exclusively for Faraway & Fine guests.

Your Cultural Director, a former curator at the Danish Design Centre, arranges a private after-hours walk through the Designmuseum's permanent collection, a day trip to Kronborg Castle (Shakespeare's Elsinore) in Helsingør, and a private dinner prepared by a New Nordic chef in your townhouse using market produce from Torvehallerne.

  • 5 nights in a private townhouse, Frederiksstaden, Copenhagen
  • After-hours private tour of Designmuseum Denmark
  • Day trip to Kronborg Castle, Helsingør with private guide
  • Private New Nordic dinner in-house by a visiting chef
  • Morning market visit to Torvehallerne with your Cultural Director
  • Private canal tour of Copenhagen's historic waterways
£3,900 per person · based on 2 guests

Sweden · Nordic Gastronomy

Sweden New Nordic
Gastronomy Journey

7 Nights · April to October

A journey structured entirely around the New Nordic food movement — the culinary philosophy of hyper-local, foraged, and fermented ingredients that transformed world cuisine from a Stockholm kitchen. Faraway & Fine's Gastronomy Director, a former sous-chef at a two-Michelin-star restaurant in Gothenburg, guides the entire week.

The itinerary moves from Stockholm to Sörmland for a guided forest foraging expedition, south to Gothenburg for a private chef's table at one of the city's Michelin-recognised restaurants, and west to Malmö for a private tasting session at a craft aquavit distillery in the city's regenerated Hyllie district.

  • Guided forest foraging in Sörmland with a professional forager
  • Private chef's table at a Michelin-recognised Gothenburg restaurant
  • Private aquavit tasting at a Malmö craft distillery
  • Home cooking session with a Swedish farmhouse family in Dalarna
  • Visits to IKEA's original Älmhult store and design archive (private access)
  • All accommodation in design-led Swedish boutique properties
£5,800 per person · based on 2 guests
