About This Cruise

Cruise FF-NOR-24A is Faraway & Fine's flagship Norwegian fjord voyage, operated aboard the MV Nordlys — a 38-metre expedition ketch purpose-restored for Geirangerfjord waters. The route covers two of Norway's UNESCO World Heritage fjords and includes a private guided ascent to the Seven Sisters waterfall viewpoint, accessible only on foot from the water's edge.

Departures run weekly between May and September 2025. The May and June departures offer the best combination of high waterfall flow (from snowmelt) and long evenings. July and August offer the warmest temperatures and midnight sun conditions. September departures have the lightest tourist traffic and the first possibility of autumn colour on the upper slopes.

The MV Nordlys carries a maximum of six guests. All cabins are below-deck with either a porthole or a deck-level window. There is no interior cabin — all cabins have natural light. The main saloon, rebuilt in Norwegian oak during the 2022 restoration, runs the full beam of the vessel and contains the dining table, a wood-burning fireplace, and a small reference library of Norwegian natural history.

MV Nordlys

Built in Bergen in 1987 as an offshore supply vessel, MV Nordlys was acquired by Faraway & Fine in 2021 and comprehensively restored at a shipyard in Ålesund over 14 months. Her hull is ice-rated to Class 1C. She carries up to 6 guests in 5 cabins with a crew of 5: captain, first mate, engineer, chef, and expedition guide.

The main saloon has been rebuilt in reclaimed Norwegian oak and features a wood-burning Jøtul fireplace, a dining table seating 10, and a selection of Norwegian natural history and navigation charts. The sundeck is partially covered and heated by a radiant panel, allowing deck dining in most weather conditions.
