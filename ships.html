The Fleet

Our Three
Vessels

Faraway & Fine™ operates three privately chartered expedition vessels, each purpose-built or restored for Scandinavian waters. No vessel carries more than eight guests.

Fjord Expeditions · Norway

MV Nordlys

Built Bergen, 1987 · Restored 2022 · 38 metres

The MV Nordlys is the flagship of the Faraway & Fine fleet. Originally a supply vessel for the Norwegian oil industry, she was purchased by Faraway & Fine in 2021 and comprehensively restored at a shipyard in Ålesund over 14 months. Her hull is ice-rated to Class 1C, making her suitable for operations in the fjords year-round and in southern Norwegian waters in winter.

She accommodates up to six guests across three cabins, each with an en-suite bathroom and panoramic porthole. The main saloon has been entirely redesigned with reclaimed Norwegian oak and a wood-burning fireplace. The sundeck is heated and covers the full beam of the vessel.

Archipelago Sailing · Sweden

SY Isfjord

Built Gothenburg, 2004 · Refitted 2020 · 28 metres

The SY Isfjord is a traditional ketch-rigged sailing yacht, designed by Swedish naval architect Per Brohäll and built at the Hallberg-Rassy yard in Gothenburg. She is the most manoeuvrable vessel in the fleet, able to navigate shallow channels in the Stockholm and Bohuslän archipelagos that are inaccessible to larger boats.

She carries up to four guests in two double cabins. The galley has been upgraded to a full chef's kitchen, and her teak deck has been entirely replaced. She is the only sailing vessel in the fleet — weather permitting, all passages are made under sail.

Arctic Expeditions · Northern Norway

MV Kvitbjørn

Built Tromsø, 1998 · Upgraded 2019 · 44 metres

MV Kvitbjørn ("White Bear") is the most robust vessel in the fleet, originally built as a research ship for the University of Tromsø's Arctic biology department. She is ice-strengthened to Class 1B and carries a full complement of Arctic expedition equipment including a RIB launch, inflatable kayaks, and a polar bear watch station on the bow.

She accommodates up to eight guests across four cabins. A dedicated equipment room on the lower deck houses cold-weather gear for all guests. A roof observation deck fitted with a retractable windscreen is the finest northern lights viewing platform in the Faraway & Fine fleet.
